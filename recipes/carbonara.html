Pasta Carbonara

Discover how to make superb spaghetti carbonara

This cheesy pasta dish is an Italian favourite and with the right technique, you can make it perfect every time.

In this real spaghetti carbonara we dont use any cream, thats strictly forbidden.

Ingredients

  • Pancetta
  • Pecorino cheese
  • Parmesan
  • Eggs
  • Spaghetti
  • Garlic cloves
  • Sea salt and ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Put a large saucepan of water on to boil.
  2. Finely chop the 100g pancetta, having first removed any rind. Finely grate 50g pecorino cheese and 50 g parmesan and mix them together.
  3. Beat the 3 large eggs in a medium bowl and season with a litte freshly grated black pepper. Set everything aside.
  4. Add 1 tsp salt to the boiling water, add 350g spaghetti and when the water comes back to the boil, cook at a constant simmer, covered, for 10 minutes or until al dente.
  5. Squash 2 peeled plump garlic cloves with the blade of a knife, just to bruise it.
  6. While the spaghetti is cooking, fry the pancetta with the garlic. Drop 50g unsalted butter into a large frying pan or wok and, as soon as the butter has melted, tip in the pancetta and garlic.
  7. Leave the cook on medium heat for about 5 minutes, stirring often, until the pancetta is golden and crisp. The garlic has now imparted its flavour, sp take it out with a slotted spoon and discard.
  8. Keep the heat under the pancetta on low. When the pasta is ready, lift it from the water with a pasta fork or thongs and put it in the frying pan with the pancetta. Don't worry if a little water drops in the pan as well and don't throw the pasta water away yet.
  9. Mix most of the cheese in with the eggs, keeping a small handful back for sprinkling over later.
  10. Take the pan of spaghetti and pancetta off the heat. Now quickly pour in the eggs and cheese. Using the thongs or a long fork, lift up the spaghetti so it mixes easily with the egg mixture, wich thickens but doesn't scramble, and everything is coated.
  11. Add extra pasta cooking water to keep it saucy. You don't want it wet, just moist. Season with a little salt, if needed.
  12. Use a long-pronged fork to twist the pasta on to the serving plate.
